Extended Reality

Extended reality, or XR, covers everything that falls under Augmented Reality, Virtual Reality, Mixed Reality, or anything in between AR and VR.

Edge Computing

Edge computing is a distributed computing pattern that brings data storage and computation closer to the data source, thereby improving the response time and saving the bandwidth.
3

5G / Wifi 6

5G networks are estimated to have more than 1.7 billion subscribers across the globe. 
4

Blockchain

Blockchain is one of the contemporary technologies with a bright future. Blockchain is expected to reach USD 19.9 billion with a CAGR (compound annual growth rate) of 42.8%.
5

Cybersecurity (Digital Trust)

The global cybersecurity market size is expected to reach 31.82 billion USD by the end of 2023, with a CAGR (compound annual growth rate) of 15.2%. 
6

Robotics And Automation

Robotics is a significant industry that is gaining momentum with time.  
7

IoT (Internet of Things)

IoT devices are increasing by almost 31 percent every year, and according to reports, there will be 30 billion IoT devices by 2022. 
8

Quantum Computing

Quantum Computing uses the properties of quantum physics to perform calculations and simulations that would not be possible on a traditional machine.
9

Web 3.0

Decentralized cryptocurrency networks must ensure that transactions are carried out safely and securely without the involvement of a centralized organization like Visa or PayPal.

AI and AIaaS (AI as a service)

AI or Artificial Intelligence is the top most trending technology these days. It enables machines to think and act like humans.
