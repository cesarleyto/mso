Lesson 1:

What is Technology?

Defining Tech

Technology is the application of science to solve a problem.

Technology is not just machines.

The word "technology" comes from the greek word "techne" which means "art" or "craft". Based on the etymology, the term refers to the art or craft of responding to our needs.

Technology helps solve our needs.

Spectrum

1

INVENTION

The act of bringing ideas or objects together in a novel way to create something that did not exist before.

Keyword:
Pioneer ideas
2

INNOVATION

An innovation is the implementation of a new or significantly changed product or process. 

Keyword:
Improvement ideas
3

IMITATION

Imitation is all about using the already developed things in one's own way. 

Keyword:
Customized ideas

The main problem with invention and innovation is that there is a huge amount of cost which is put into to develop new things whereas imitation just uses the benefits of the already developed things.

Design process

There are many research models to find an innovative solution. In essentials, the steps in most methodologies are pretty similar.

1

DEFINE THE PROBLEM

Find something that is wrong or something that could be improved. Discuss the problem with your team or someone else. Identify the size and effect of the problem. 

Output:
A clear definition of the opportunity and a goal for fixing it.
2

BRAINSTORM SOLUTIONS

Get creative, research and generate potential solutions. Use your imagination.

Output:
Pick-list of possible solutions that would achieve the stated goal.
3

PICK A SOLUTION

Get serious.
Evualuate the effectiveness and practicality of solutions and decide upon a course of action.

Output:
Decision of what solution you will implement.
4

SKETCH THE SOLUTION

Put your concept down.
Sketch, draw, write, build or do whatever you need to do to figure out how your idea is gonna be executed. Do it till you find your definitive concept.

Output:
A defined idea of your solution that will lead your prototyping process or making/implementation process.
5

IMPLEMENT THE SOLUTION

Make the best prototype you can build or the best implementation you can do based on your sketching process.

Output:
A useful prototype.
An implemented solution.
6

REFLECT

Review the effectiveness of the solutions.
How well did it work?
Will it work in the future?
Is it good enough?

Output:
Insights; case-studies; project resume.
7

ARE YOU DONE?

Is your final product polish enough?
Can you improve it?

Do a learning process to find solutions to those problems you have found in your design.
